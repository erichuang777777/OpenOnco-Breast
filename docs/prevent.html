Prevention / Early-Diagnosis Risk Assessment

HCP-mediated tool. Fill in the at-risk individual's profile below. The system identifies prevention-eligible risk factors (IARC Group 1 infectious carcinogens, hereditary pedigree red flags, occupational exposures, chronic conditions, iatrogenic + lifestyle) and generates a structured Prevention Plan with two tracks (standard intervention + observation alternative) per CHARTER §15.2 C4. Not a medical device; always verify with treating clinician.

A. Patient demographics
B. Chronic infections (IARC Group 1)

Each elevates risk of specific cancers; intervention is curative or risk-reducing antiviral / antibiotic / vaccine therapy.

C. Family pedigree (hereditary cancer red flags)

Computed flags. Pedigree-to-criteria evaluator is a v0.2-C engine extension (currently expects flags pre-computed by clinician).

D. Confirmed germline carriers

For individuals with a known pathogenic variant on prior testing — routes to syndrome-specific carrier surveillance.

D. Lifestyle & environmental risk factors
E. Occupational exposures (IARC Group 1)
F. Chronic medical conditions
G. Iatrogenic risk factors (prior treatment exposures)
H. Reproductive history (hormonal context)
I. Universal cross-cutting flags

Broad-spectrum risk flags that apply across multiple disease groups — augment disease-specific RFs above.

Prevention RedFlags that will fire:

    Each fired RF routes to a 2-track Prevention Plan (intervention + observation per CHARTER §15.2 C4). Click Open in Plan Builder to run the engine and see the full output.

    CHARTER §15 C1 invariant: OpenOnco is an HCP-mediated tool, not a patient-direct medical device. This questionnaire is for clinician-led prevention consultations. All content is STUB pending two-Clinical-Co-Lead signoff per §6.1 dev-mode exemption.

    Data: nothing here is sent to a server. All processing happens client-side once you click Open in Plan Builder (which embeds the OpenOnco engine via Pyodide).

    Coverage: 50+ prevention pilots across 7 risk-factor categories (infectious / hereditary / chronic-condition / occupational / iatrogenic / lifestyle / reproductive) per IARC Monographs + USPSTF + NCCN Genetic/Familial High-Risk + AGA + AASLD.
