Red Flag Origin

DefinitionPrimary Sjögren's syndrome (chronic autoimmune exocrinopathy with characteristic anti-Ro/SSA ± anti-La/SSB autoantibodies and histologic focal lymphocytic infiltration of minor salivary glands; EULAR/ACR 2016 classification criteria) in an individual without a current lymphoma diagnosis. The strongest hematologic-cancer risk factor among rheumatic diseases — ~15-20x relative risk for marginal zone (MALT) lymphoma, predominantly arising in parotid and lacrimal glands, with lifetime incidence ~5-10%. Risk concentrated in patients with persistent parotid swelling, palpable purpura, cryoglobulinemia, low C4 complement, monoclonal gammopathy, lymph- node enlargement, or high EULAR Sjögren's Disease Activity Index (ESSDAI). Notes

v0.3 chronic-condition prevention pilot — Sjögren's syndrome / MALT lymphoma. Prevention-persona RedFlag (CHARTER §3 amended 2026-05-18 Path A, HCP-mediated). Fires when patient profile shows confirmed primary Sjögren's syndrome AND no confirmed lymphoma. Engine routes to PreventionPlan with 2 tracks: annual physical exam + lab monitoring + parotid US for any new/persistent swelling (IND-SJOGREN-MALT-LYMPHOMA-PREVENTION-SURVEILLANCE) as standard; rheumatology-driven observation with lymphoma workup only on clinical suspicion (IND-SJOGREN-MALT-LYMPHOMA-PREVENTION-OBSERVATION) as alternative. Evidence base: - **Lymphoma risk magnitude:** Theander et al. Ann Rheum Dis 2006 (Swedish cohort, SIR ~15.6); Solans-Laqué et al. Semin Arthritis Rheum 2011 (meta-analysis, RR ~13-19). MALT lymphoma comprises ~60-70% of Sjögren-related lymphomas; DLBCL ~20-25%; nodal marginal zone + others remainder. - **Predictive features:** Brito-Zerón et al. Ann Rheum Dis 2014 (multicenter cohort) — predictors: persistent salivary swelling (OR ~3), low C4 (OR ~2.5), cryoglobulinemia (OR ~2.5), palpable purpura (OR ~2), monoclonal gammopathy (OR ~2).
